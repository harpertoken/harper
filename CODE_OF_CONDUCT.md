# Code of Conduct

[![Contributor Covenant](https://img.shields.io/badge/Contributor%20Covenant-2.1-4baaaa.svg)](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)

Harper is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free environment for all contributors, users, and community members. We value diversity and believe that everyone deserves to participate without fear of discrimination or harassment.

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- Age
- Body size
- Visible or invisible disability
- Ethnicity
- Sex characteristics
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Education
- Socio-economic status
- Nationality
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Caste
- Color
- Religion
- Sexual identity and orientation

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

### Positive Behavior

- **Respectful Communication**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
- **Constructive Feedback**: Providing helpful, actionable feedback
- **Collaboration**: Working together effectively and respectfully
- **Empathy**: Being considerate of others' feelings and perspectives
- **Professionalism**: Maintaining appropriate conduct in all interactions
- **Inclusivity**: Ensuring everyone feels welcome and valued

### Community Guidelines

- **Be respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- **Give and gracefully accept** constructive feedback
- **Accept responsibility** and apologize for mistakes
- **Focus on what is best** for the community
- **Show empathy** towards other community members
- **Use inclusive language** and avoid discriminatory remarks

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable and will not be tolerated:

### Harassment and Discrimination

- **Harassment**: Any form of unwelcome conduct, including:
  - Offensive verbal comments, jokes, or language
  - Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
  - Harassing photography or recording
  - Sustained disruption of discussions or events
  - Inappropriate physical contact
  - Unwelcome sexual attention or advances

- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory behavior based on protected characteristics

### Inappropriate Content

- **Sexual Content**: Displaying or linking to sexualized content
- **Violence**: Threats of violence or violent language
- **Hate Speech**: Derogatory comments or hate speech
- **Misinformation**: Deliberately spreading false information

### Disruptive Behavior

- **Trolling**: Deliberately inflammatory or provocative behavior
- **Spam**: Excessive or irrelevant messaging
- **Doxxing**: Publishing private information without consent
- **Impersonation**: Pretending to be someone else

### Technical Violations

- **Security Issues**: Attempting to compromise system security
- **Malware**: Distributing malicious code or software
- **Spam**: Automated messaging or excessive posting

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Contact the maintainers** immediately
2. **Provide details** of the incident
3. **Preserve evidence** if possible (screenshots, links, etc.)
4. **Allow time** for investigation

### Reporting Methods

- **Email**: harpertoken@icloud.com
- **GitHub Issue**: [File a private report](https://github.com/harpertoken/harper/security/advisories/new)
- **Direct Message**: Contact maintainers through available channels

### What to Include

When reporting an incident, please provide:

- **Description**: What happened and when
- **Parties Involved**: Who was involved (use usernames/handles)
- **Evidence**: Screenshots, links, or other supporting information
- **Impact**: How the behavior affected you or others
- **Witnesses**: Other people who saw or were affected by the incident

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion. Reporter identity will be kept confidential unless explicitly authorized otherwise.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

### Maintainers' Duties

Community leaders are responsible for:

- **Clarifying standards** of acceptable behavior
- **Taking appropriate action** in response to violations
- **Removing, editing, or rejecting** contributions that don't align with this Code of Conduct
- **Communicating reasons** for moderation decisions when appropriate

### Enforcement Principles

- **Fair and Consistent**: Apply standards equally to all community members
- **Transparent**: Explain actions and reasoning when possible
- **Educational**: Help violators understand and improve
- **Protective**: Prioritize the safety and well-being of community members

## Enforcement Guidelines

### Investigation Process

1. **Receive Report**: Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. **Investigate**: Gather information and evidence
3. **Determine Violation**: Assess if Code of Conduct was violated
4. **Apply Consequences**: Choose appropriate response
5. **Communicate**: Inform relevant parties of the outcome
6. **Follow Up**: Monitor situation and prevent recurrence

### Consequences

Consequences for violations may include:

#### Minor Violations
- **Warning**: Private or public warning with explanation
- **Correction**: Request to modify or remove inappropriate content
- **Temporary Restriction**: Short-term suspension from certain activities

#### Serious Violations
- **Suspension**: Temporary ban from community participation
- **Permanent Ban**: Removal from all community spaces
- **Legal Action**: Involving authorities for criminal behavior

### Appeals Process

Community members may appeal enforcement decisions by:

1. **Contacting maintainers** with appeal reasoning
2. **Providing additional context** or evidence
3. **Requesting review** of the decision

Appeals will be considered fairly and decisions may be reversed if warranted.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- **All community spaces**: GitHub repositories, issues, discussions, pull requests
- **Official communications**: Documentation, websites, social media
- **Community events**: Meetups, conferences, or online events
- **Related activities**: Any activity representing the Harper project

### Out of Scope

This Code of Conduct does not apply to:
- **Third-party platforms**: Unless officially representing Harper
- **Private communications**: Personal emails or direct messages
- **Unrelated activities**: Personal conduct not connected to the project

## Contact Information

- **Email**: harpertoken@icloud.com
- **GitHub Issues**: [Report conduct issues](https://github.com/harpertoken/harper/issues)
